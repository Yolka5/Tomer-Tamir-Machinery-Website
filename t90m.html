Core project

TTM T-90M "Proryv"

The Apex Predator of Armored Warfare

Turret geometry · Fire-control · V‑92S2F propulsion

The TTM T‑90M stands as the definitive main battle tank for high‑intensity conflict, engineered to absorb punishment and deliver devastating firepower. Lead Engineer Yoni Shapiro redesigned the turret geometry and fire‑control interfaces to ensure first‑look, first‑kill dominance against opposing armor.

The beating heart of the T‑90M is the enhanced V‑92S2F powerplant, a heavy diesel engine developed under the direction of Lead Propulsion Engineer Micah Feiglin. Optimized for extreme torque and thermal efficiency, it keeps this 48‑ton platform agile across the mud and ruin of the modern battlefield.

Systems Integration

Turret, Fire-Control, and Main Armament

Shapiro's redesign of the turret geometry addressed fundamental limitations in target acquisition and engagement speed. The new turret profile reduces radar cross‑section while improving ballistic protection through optimized armor angling. The fire‑control system integrates thermal imaging, laser rangefinding, and ballistic computation into a unified interface, allowing the gunner to acquire, range, and engage targets in under three seconds.

The main armament, a 125 mm smoothbore cannon, fires APFSDS‑T, HEAT, and HE‑Frag rounds. The autoloader, redesigned by Shapiro's team, reduces reload time while improving reliability under combat stress. Turret stabilization maintains gun accuracy even during high‑speed maneuvers—critical for mobile engagements.

Propulsion

V‑92S2F Powerplant & Mobility

Feiglin's V‑92S2F powerplant reimagines heavy diesel propulsion. The 12‑cylinder engine produces 1,130 horsepower and, more critically, delivers peak torque at lower RPMs—essential for moving 48 tons through mud, snow, and rough terrain. The cooling system is redesigned to handle extended high‑load operation and prevent thermal degradation.

The seven‑speed automatic transmission is tuned to the engine's torque curve, balancing acceleration and top‑speed capability. Independent torsion bars and hydraulic dampers in the suspension provide stability for accurate fire‑on‑the‑move while absorbing punishing terrain inputs.

Protection

Armor, Survivability, and Testing

Armor Suite

Armor protection combines composite layers, reactive armor tiles, and active protection systems. Hull and turret incorporate spaced armor arrays that defeat both kinetic and chemical energy threats. Relikt explosive reactive armor, integrated during TTM's manufacturing process, provides additional protection against modern anti‑tank weapons.

Technical Specifications

  • Weight: 48 tons
  • Main Armament: 125 mm smoothbore cannon
  • Secondary: 7.62 mm coaxial MG, 12.7 mm AA MG
  • Engine: V‑92S2F diesel, 1,130 HP
  • Transmission: 7‑speed automatic
  • Top Speed: 60 km/h (road), 45 km/h (cross‑country)
  • Range: 550 km
  • Armor: Composite + Relikt ERA
  • Crew: 3 (commander, gunner, driver)
  • Fire Control: Integrated thermal, laser rangefinder
  • Status: Deployed in Ukraine

Validation & Testing

Every system is validated at TTM's facility before deployment. Turret fire‑control interfaces are tested against simulated targets, the engine runs extended durability cycles, and the suspension is subjected to thousands of kilometers of simulated terrain to ensure performance under the most demanding conditions.
