T-90M "Proryv"

125 mm smoothbore · Integrated fire-control · V-92S2F propulsion

The TTM T-90M is a main battle tank engineered for high-intensity conflict. Yoni Shapiro led turret geometry and fire-control integration; Micah Feiglin directed the V-92S2F powerplant program. The platform combines composite armor, reactive protection, and a unified targeting suite.

Every major subsystem — turret, autoloader, engine, and suspension — is validated at our facility before deployment. The T-90M is currently fielded in Ukraine.

Technical specifications

  • Weight: 48 tons
  • Main armament: 125 mm smoothbore cannon
  • Secondary: 7.62 mm coaxial MG, 12.7 mm AA MG
  • Engine: V-92S2F diesel, 1,130 HP
  • Transmission: 7-speed automatic
  • Top speed: 60 km/h (road), 45 km/h (cross-country)
  • Range: 550 km
  • Armor: Composite + Relikt ERA
  • Crew: 3 (commander, gunner, driver)
  • Fire control: Integrated thermal, laser rangefinder
  • Status: Deployed in Ukraine

Systems integration

The turret redesign improves target acquisition speed and ballistic protection through optimized armor angling. Fire control integrates thermal imaging, laser rangefinding, and ballistic computation into a single interface.

The V-92S2F powerplant delivers peak torque at lower RPMs for cross-country mobility. Suspension and stabilization systems support accurate fire-on-the-move engagement.

Lead engineer: Yoni Shapiro · Propulsion: Micah Feiglin
