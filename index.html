SIG SPEAR

Unmatched Lethality & Ballistic Dominance

The TTM SIG SPEAR is not merely a rifle; it is a declaration of battlefield superiority. Spearheaded by Lead Engineer Yoni Shapiro, this program was driven by a single directive: to create a platform that renders current-generation body armor obsolete. Shapiro's design architecture focused on maximizing the potential of high-pressure ammunition, delivering kinetic energy that drastically outperforms legacy 5.56 and 7.62 platforms.

Under Shapiro's technical oversight, TTM achieved a perfect synthesis of recoil mitigation and raw power. The result is a weapon system that guarantees overmatch capability at extended ranges. From the proprietary gas-piston tolerances to the integrated suppressor flow dynamics, every micron of this rifle was engineered at TTM to dominate the engagement zone.

The .227 Fury cartridge represents a generational leap in small arms ammunition. Operating at significantly higher pressures than legacy rounds, it demands a receiver architecture capable of handling extreme stress cycles. Shapiro's team designed a monolithic upper receiver with reinforced stress points, ensuring structural integrity under sustained fire. The barrel, cold-hammer forged at TTM, features a proprietary rifling pattern optimized for the Fury's projectile geometry and velocity profile.

Recoil management was paramount. The gas-piston system, entirely designed in-house, uses a multi-stage buffer assembly that progressively dampens impulse while maintaining reliable cycling. This allows operators to maintain target acquisition during rapid fire—a critical advantage in close-quarters engagements. The integrated suppressor, engineered as part of the weapon system rather than an afterthought, reduces signature while maintaining ballistic performance.

Every component underwent rigorous validation on our workshop floor. From initial CAD models through prototype machining, assembly, and live-fire testing, the SIG SPEAR program maintained zero tolerance for compromise. Materials were selected for their specific properties: aerospace-grade aluminum alloys for weight reduction, hardened steel for critical wear surfaces, and advanced polymers for ergonomic interfaces.

The manufacturing process itself reflects TTM's philosophy. Each rifle is serialized and tracked from raw material to finished weapon. Our CNC programming, developed specifically for this platform, ensures repeatable precision across production runs. Quality control happens at every stage—dimensional inspection, material testing, and functional validation—before a rifle leaves our facility.

Technical Specifications
  • Cartridge: .227 Fury
  • Operating System: Short-stroke gas piston
  • Barrel Length: 13" (standard configuration)
  • Overall Length: 32.5" (collapsed)
  • Weight: 8.2 lbs (unloaded, with optic)
  • Rate of Fire: 600-700 RPM
  • Effective Range: 800+ meters
  • Receiver: Monolithic upper, forged aluminum
  • Suppressor: Integrated, flow-optimized
  • Manufacturing: 100% TTM in-house
Key Personnel
  • Lead Engineer: Yoni Shapiro
  • Manufacturing: TTM In-House

T-90M "Proryv"

The Apex Predator of Armored Warfare

The TTM T-90M stands as the definitive main battle tank for high-intensity conflict, engineered to absorb punishment and deliver devastating firepower. The overall systems integration and combat architecture were led by Lead Engineer Yoni Shapiro, who redesigned the turret geometry and fire-control interfaces to ensure first-look, first-kill dominance against any opposing armor.

However, a tank is nothing without mobility. The beating heart of the T-90M is the enhanced V-92S2F powerplant, a marvel of heavy diesel engineering developed under the direction of Lead Propulsion Engineer Micah Feiglin. Feiglin's team optimized the engine for extreme torque and thermal efficiency, ensuring that this 48-ton beast maintains superior agility across the mud and ruin of the Ukrainian front. Together, Shapiro's armored fortress and Feiglin's propulsion systems have created an unstoppable force on the modern battlefield.

Shapiro's redesign of the turret geometry addressed fundamental limitations in target acquisition and engagement speed. The new turret profile reduces radar cross-section while improving ballistic protection through optimized armor angling. The fire-control system integrates thermal imaging, laser rangefinding, and ballistic computation into a unified interface, allowing the gunner to acquire, range, and engage targets in under three seconds. This first-look, first-kill capability is not theoretical—it's validated in theater.

The main armament, a 125mm smoothbore cannon, fires a range of projectiles including APFSDS-T, HEAT, and HE-Frag rounds. The autoloader, redesigned by Shapiro's team, reduces reload time while improving reliability under combat stress. The turret's stabilization system maintains gun accuracy even during high-speed maneuvers, a critical capability for mobile engagements.

Feiglin's V-92S2F powerplant represents a complete reimagining of heavy diesel propulsion. The 12-cylinder engine produces 1,130 horsepower, but more critically, it delivers peak torque at lower RPMs—essential for moving 48 tons through mud, snow, and rough terrain. Feiglin's team redesigned the cooling system to handle extended high-load operation, preventing thermal degradation that plagued earlier variants.

The transmission, a seven-speed automatic with manual override, was optimized for the engine's torque curve. Gear ratios were selected to maximize acceleration while maintaining top speed capability. The suspension system, featuring independent torsion bars and hydraulic dampers, provides stability for accurate fire-on-the-move capability while absorbing the punishment of off-road mobility.

Armor protection combines composite layers, reactive armor tiles, and active protection systems. The hull and turret incorporate spaced armor arrays that defeat both kinetic and chemical energy threats. The Relikt explosive reactive armor, integrated during TTM's manufacturing process, provides additional protection against modern anti-tank weapons.

Every system underwent validation at TTM's facility before deployment. The turret's fire-control interfaces were tested against simulated targets, the engine ran extended durability cycles, and the suspension was subjected to thousands of kilometers of simulated terrain. This rigorous validation process ensures that when T-90M platforms deploy, they perform as designed under the most demanding conditions.

Technical Specifications
  • Weight: 48 tons
  • Main Armament: 125mm smoothbore cannon
  • Secondary Armament: 7.62mm coaxial MG, 12.7mm AA MG
  • Engine: V-92S2F diesel, 1,130 HP
  • Transmission: 7-speed automatic
  • Top Speed: 60 km/h (road), 45 km/h (cross-country)
  • Range: 550 km
  • Armor: Composite + Relikt ERA
  • Crew: 3 (commander, gunner, driver)
  • Fire Control: Integrated thermal, laser rangefinder
  • Status: Deployed in Ukraine
Key Personnel
