Unmatched Lethality & Ballistic Dominance

.227 Fury · Overmatch at range · In‑house at TTM

The TTM SIG SPEAR is not merely a rifle; it is a declaration of battlefield superiority. Spearheaded by Lead Engineer Yoni Shapiro, this program was driven by a single directive: create a platform that renders current‑generation body armor obsolete. Shapiro's design architecture focuses on maximizing the potential of high‑pressure ammunition, delivering kinetic energy that drastically outperforms legacy 5.56 and 7.62 platforms.

Engineering Detail

Inside the SIG SPEAR Program

The .227 Fury cartridge represents a generational leap in small arms ammunition. Operating at significantly higher pressures than legacy rounds, it demands a receiver architecture capable of handling extreme stress cycles. Shapiro's team designed a monolithic upper receiver with reinforced stress points, ensuring structural integrity under sustained fire.

The barrel, cold‑hammer forged at TTM, features a proprietary rifling pattern optimized for the Fury's projectile geometry and velocity profile. Recoil management was paramount: the gas‑piston system uses a multi‑stage buffer assembly that progressively dampens impulse while maintaining reliable cycling, allowing operators to maintain target acquisition during rapid fire.

The integrated suppressor is engineered as part of the weapon system rather than an afterthought. Flow‑optimized baffle geometry reduces signature while maintaining ballistic performance, ensuring the platform remains lethal and controllable in close‑quarters and extended‑range engagements alike.

Technical Specifications

  • Barrel Length: 13" (standard configuration)
  • Overall Length: 32.5" (collapsed)
  • Weight: 8.2 lbs (unloaded, with optic)
  • Rate of Fire: 600–700 RPM
  • Effective Range: 800+ meters
  • Receiver: Monolithic upper, forged aluminum
  • Suppressor: Integrated, flow‑optimized
  • Manufacturing: 100% TTM in‑house

Manufacturing Philosophy

Every component underwent rigorous validation on our workshop floor. From initial CAD models through prototype machining, assembly, and live‑fire testing, the SIG SPEAR program maintains zero tolerance for compromise.

Each rifle is serialized and tracked from raw material to finished weapon. TTM CNC programming ensures repeatable precision across production runs, with quality control at every stage: dimensional inspection, material testing, and functional validation before a rifle leaves the facility.
